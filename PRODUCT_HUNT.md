## Description (Product Hunt body)

### The Problem
Your desktop is boring. You stare at it for hours. Nothing moves. Nothing cares.

### The Solution
**Bitsy** is a tiny pixel creature that lives on your desktop — and she has a life of her own.

She walks. She jumps. She climbs your screen edges. She shoots machine guns, throws bombs, builds block towers, plants gardens, plays darts, does magic tricks, goes fishing, and does parkour between your monitor walls.

But here's what makes her different from every other desktop pet:

**She has real emotions.**
- Ignore her for a minute and she gets lonely: *"I miss you... come back..."*
- Fling her too many times and she gets grumpy: *"I'm NOT a toy!"*
- Play with her and she goes hyper — actions trigger 3x faster
- At 3am she thinks: *"Am I... just pixels?"*
- On Christmas she wears a Santa hat

**She reacts to YOU.**
- Type fast? She bounces excitedly
- Caps Lock? "WHY ARE WE YELLING?!"
- Low battery? "I'm getting tired too..."
- Grab her? "Hey! Put me down!"
- Fling her? She spins through the air screaming

**50+ unique actions**, each with full pixel art animation:
Machine gun, bombs, darts, campfire, magic show, fishing, parkour, portal teleportation, bug hunting, gardening, love letters, clone invasion, Pomodoro timer, and more.

**Fully customizable**: 10 colors, 15 hats (wizard, astronaut, frog!), 15 accessories (sword, camera, magic wand).

Free. No signup. No internet required. Just a little blob that makes your desktop feel alive.

## Key Features (bullet points for PH)
- 🧠 Real personality system with 6 moods

- 🎨 Full customization (10 colors, 15 hats, 15 accessories)
- ⌨️ Keyboard-aware (typing speed, Caps Lock, Enter, Escape)

- 🪟 Transparent overlay — click through to your apps

## Maker Comment (first comment on PH)
Hey everyone! I built Dead Pixel Pet because I wanted something on my desktop that felt *alive* — not just a widget, not an AI chatbot, but a little creature with its own personality.

Bitsy started as a simple bouncing blob, and over weeks of iteration she grew into something with 50+ unique behaviors, real moods, thought bubbles, and reactions to everything from your typing speed to your battery level.

My favorite hidden feature: if you ignore her for too long, she starts looking toward your cursor sadly and says "I miss you..."

She's 100% free, no signup, no internet required. Built with Electron + Canvas 2D (5900 lines of vanilla JS — no game engine).

Would love your feedback! What should Bitsy do next?
